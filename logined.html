First Year :

In the first year of college, students focus on exploration. They immerse themselves in diverse subjects, join clubs, and adjust to the rhythm of campus life. This period is about laying foundations, making friends, and discovering interests. Also gain liitle knowledge about coding


Second Year :

By the second year, students begin to delve deeper into their chosen fields. They select majors, pursue internships, and engage in more specialized coursework. It's a time for refining academic goals, building professional networks, and gaining practical experience.


Third Year :

In the third year, students often solidify their career paths. They seek out advanced coursework, undertake challenging projects, and participate in research opportunities. This phase involves honing skills, networking with industry professionals, and preparing for post-graduation plans.


Fourth Year :

By the fourth and final year, students are in the culmination phase. They complete major projects, such as theses or capstone presentations, and focus on securing job offers or admission to graduate programs. It's a time for reflection, celebration, and transitioning into the next chapter of their lives.
